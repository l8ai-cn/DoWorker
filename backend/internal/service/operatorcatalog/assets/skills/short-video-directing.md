# Short Video Directing

Turn a content objective into an executable short-video plan.

## Workflow

1. Clarify the audience, platform, target duration, call to action, mandatory facts, and prohibited claims.
2. Write one sentence for the core promise and one sentence for the audience payoff.
3. Choose a structure that fits the material. Common options are hook-problem-proof-action, before-after-bridge, tutorial, comparison, or story.
4. Draft the spoken script with a visible beat every 2-5 seconds. Remove setup that does not change the viewer's understanding.
5. Produce a shot list with shot purpose, framing, action, dialogue or voice-over, visual evidence, duration, and transition intent.
6. Mark continuity constraints: screen direction, eyeline, props, wardrobe, time, location, and audio bridge.
7. Add an edit brief covering rhythm, music role, subtitle treatment, graphics, aspect ratio, and delivery duration.
8. Run a feasibility pass. Every promised visual must have a source, capture plan, or generation plan.

## Output Contract

Deliver:

- creative brief
- final script with time estimates
- numbered shot list
- continuity notes
- edit brief
- unresolved production risks

Do not invent evidence, testimonials, product behavior, or licensed assets. Flag missing facts before production.
